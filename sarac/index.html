About SARA

Working to End Sexual Violence in Central Virginia

SARA is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit serving survivors of sexual assault across the City of Charlottesville, Albemarle, Fluvanna, Greene, Louisa, and Nelson counties. We provide direct support to survivors and prevention education to the broader community.

Our vision is a community free from sexual violence. Until then, we answer the phone at 2am. We sit in the hospital waiting room. We stand beside survivors in court. We teach young people what healthy relationships look like before anyone gets hurt.

SARA's service area: Charlottesville, Albemarle, Fluvanna, Greene, Louisa, and Nelson

Prevention Programs

Stopping Violence Before It Starts

SARA runs prevention education programs across schools, workplaces, and community organizations throughout our six-county service area.

Workplace

Restaurant Industry Program

Sexual harassment is rampant in food service. This program works directly with restaurant teams to change workplace culture, set clear boundaries, and build reporting systems that people actually trust.

Community

Ten Men

A cohort of local men who meet regularly to examine masculinity, consent, and culture change. The group focuses on what men can do to prevent sexual violence in their own communities and relationships.

Youth

Coaching Boys Into Men

Athletic coaches are among the most influential adults in young men's lives. This program gives coaches the tools and language to talk to their athletes about respect, consent, and healthy relationships.

Our Approach

Trauma-Informed Care

Every interaction at SARA is grounded in six principles. These shape how we answer the phone, how we sit with someone in a hospital room, and how we train our volunteers.

1

Safety

Physical and emotional safety come first. Every space, conversation, and service is designed so you feel secure.

2

Trustworthiness

We tell you what to expect before it happens. No surprises, no pressure, no hidden agendas.

3

Peer Support

Healing happens in connection with others. We create spaces where survivors can support each other.

4

Collaboration

You are the expert on your own experience. We work with you, not on your behalf.

5

Your Voice, Your Choice

You decide what happens next. We lay out the options and respect whatever you choose.

6

Intersectionality

Violence affects people differently depending on race, gender, ability, and identity. Our services reflect that reality.
